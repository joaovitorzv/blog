How i’m getting a computer science education by myself

June 03, 2022

At first when I was on doing my first steps as a programmer I thought that going to a university was useless but I was quite wrong, as a self-taught developer I feel that there is a gap between those who went to a college and have not’s, but still college costs time, lot’s of money and at the end you might end up not using everything you’ve learned there on a real world job.

That’s where https://teachyourselfcs.com comes to action, it’s a guide that covers all the topics that you’ll might actually use as software engineer

  • Programming
  • Computer Architecture
  • Algorithms and Data Structures
  • Math (for cs)
  • OS, Networking, Databases
  • Languages and Compilers
  • Distributed Systems

Even if some topics is not used on a web developer day-to-day job — like Compilers or Algorithms — I personally feel that it gives me a different point of view of how things work and eventually helping me to handle any sort of problem I might encounter in my career.

Currently i’m spending about 2 hours of my days to watch and solve problems related to the topic i’m at and every topic has a recommendation of a video lecture or book, on the date of this post i’m following the Programming guide and it’s recommendation is the Brian Harvey’s SICP lectures where SICP is the Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs book also known as the "Wizard Book" in hacker culture.

I started about 1 week ago and I feel really optimistic about the knowledge I will “unlock” at the end and hope i’ts worth the effort.

🔥 If you’re interested in following this guide you can understand more at https://teachyourselfcs.com
